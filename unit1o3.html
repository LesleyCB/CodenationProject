Unit 1: Outcome 3

Unit 1, Outcome 3 (Review)

Develop and test solutions to improve ongoing use of IT tools and systems

3.1 Review the benefits and drawbacks of IT tools and systems used in terms of productivity and efficiency – could you have been more efficient when making your website? How? Could you have done it in teams?

I think I could be more efficient with my time in the future, as I have more experience using HTML and CSS and would not need to spend so much time making mistakes and correcting them.
In terms of tools and systems - the tools were fit for purpose.
I would ensure I had more experience of using the code before I took on a project utilising it in the real world. This was a steep learning curve - but the way the course is designed.

3.2 Describe ways to improve productivity and efficiency

Better time management.
Using Trello to break down the task and put time deadlines on them.
More experience and practice prior to a project.
More research and knowledge about CSS, Flexiboxes and design features prior to a project.
More research on accessibility.

3.3 Develop solutions to improve own productivity using IT in digital projects – what would you do differently next time? Team work? Use of Slack?

If I were working on another webpage project and we were doing it as a team task we would need to agree on the structure/where the content would lie/have agreed templates prior to working individually.
I would plan my time differently - and allow for certain CSS/design elements to take more time.
I would do a more detailed Trello board with deadlines for design and content elements/checklists on the cards.

3.4 Describe how you would go about testing digital solutions


It is useful to test digital products/solutions on a variety of PCs, Apple Macs, Tablets, Ipads, Laptops, Iphones and Android phones.
It is useful to test webpages on different browsers too. Online resources/websites may behave differently on a variety of browsers/devices.
This testing should take place while you are developing a project - you should be doing the trouble-shooting throughout so you know if something is not suitable for purpose.
If you have a team, you could divide the testing up - but you should agree on how something should work, so you know when the solution deviates from that/doesn't work.
It is useful to keep screen shots/records of errors/problems in case you can't reproduce them at a later date. So you can work out causes/solutions.
It is useful to test from different locations too, over different networks.
You could also have a focus group at the research and development stage to test the product and feed back any problem areas or anything they would like added or improved.
